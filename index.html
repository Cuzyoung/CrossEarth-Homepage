Abstract

Cross-domain generalization aims to extrapolate modelsâ€™ in-domain ability to diverse unseen domains. This capability is crucial for Remote Sensing (RS) imagery characterized by variabilities, such as wavelength range, modality, and platform, which leads to diverse and severe domain gaps. Therefore, Remote Sensing Domain Generalization (RSDG) has become a vital and valuable research frontier, focusing on developing generalizable models to be effectively applied to diverse scenarios. However, existing RS research has largely overlooked this area: (1) Current cross-domain models mainly focus on Domain Adaptation (DA), which adapts models to specific domains rather than diverse, unseen ones; (2) There are only a few RSDG works and both of them are specialized models trapped in overfitting and underfitting issues; (3) Existing RS Foundation Models prioritize performance on in-domain data rather than emphasizing cross-domain generalization. Considering that the universal understanding ability of foundation models inherently aligns with the concept of generalization, we focus on the semantic segmentation task and propose the first Vision Foundation Model for RSDG, CrossEarth, which equips strong cross-domain generalizability through data-level Earth-Style Injection pipeline and model-level Multi-Task Training pipeline. Additionally, given the lack of unified benchmarks in RSDG, we also curate a benchmark collection for facilitating generalizability testing in future RSDG research. The collection includes 28 semantic segmentation benchmarks centering on diverse domain gaps and segmentation tasks. Extensive experiments are conducted on collected benchmarks, demonstrating the superiority of CrossEarth.
